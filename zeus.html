ZEUS

Zeus is the supreme deity in Greek mythology, ruling as King of the Gods, ruler of Mount Olympus, and god of the sky, weather, thunder, and justice. Son of Titans Cronus and Rhea, he overthrew his father to lead the Olympians. Known for his lightning bolt weapon and amorous nature, he is married to Hera but had numerous divine and mortal lovers.

  • Roles & Attributes: As the "Cloud Gatherer" he controls the weather, maintains law and order, and acts as a protector of guests and travelers (Xenios). His symbols include the thunderbolt, eagle, bull, and oak tree.
  • Family & Origin: He is the youngest child of Cronus and Rhea, saved from being swallowed by his father when his mother hid him on Crete. He is brother to Poseidon, Hades, Hera, Demeter, and Hestia.
  • Children: Father to many gods and heroes, including Athena, Apollo, Artemis, Ares, Hermes, Dionysus, Heracles, and Perseus.
