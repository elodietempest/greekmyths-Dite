POSEIDON

Poseidon is the god of the sea, earthquakes, storms, and horses in Greek mythology. Brother of Zeus and Hades, he is one of the twelve Olympian gods. Known for his powerful trident, he rules over oceans, lakes, and rivers, and is worshipped by sailors and fishermen. Poseidon was often portrayed as moody and temperamental, capable of both granting safe passage and unleashing devastating storms.

  • Roles & Attributes: God of the sea and waters, controller of storms, earthquakes, and horses. His symbols include the trident, horse, dolphin, and bull.
  • Family & Origin: Son of Titans Cronus and Rhea, brother to Zeus, Hades, Hera, Demeter, and Hestia. He was swallowed by Cronus at birth and later rescued by his siblings.
  • Children: Father of Triton, Polyphemus, Theseus, and other mortal and divine offspring.
