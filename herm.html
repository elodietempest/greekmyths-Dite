HERMES

Hermes is the messenger god in Greek mythology and one of the twelve Olympian gods. Known for his speed and cleverness, he serves as the messenger of the gods and the guide of souls to the underworld. Hermes is also the god of travelers, merchants, trade, thieves, and communication. He is often depicted wearing winged sandals and carrying a staff called the caduceus.

  • Roles & Attributes: Messenger of the gods, protector of travelers and merchants, god of communication, trade, and cunning. His symbols include the caduceus, winged sandals, winged helmet, and tortoise.
  • Family & Origin: Son of Zeus and Maia, one of the Pleiades. Hermes showed his intelligence and trickery even as an infant.
  • Children: Father of Pan, Hermaphroditus, Abderus, and several other figures in Greek mythology.
