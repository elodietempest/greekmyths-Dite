ASCLEPIUS

Asclepius is the Greek demigod of medicine and healing. He is the son of Apollo and the mortal woman Coronis. Renowned for his extraordinary healing abilities, Asclepius could cure disease and even bring the dead back to life, which eventually led Zeus to strike him down to maintain the balance between mortals and gods. He became a symbol of medical knowledge and was worshipped widely in healing sanctuaries called Asclepieia.

  • Roles & Attributes: Demigod of medicine, healing, and medicinal knowledge. His symbols include the rod of Asclepius, snakes, and healing herbs.
  • Family & Origin: Son of Apollo and Coronis.
  • Myth: Famous for his miraculous healing powers and the Asclepieia temples where people sought cures.
