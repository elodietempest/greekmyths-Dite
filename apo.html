APOLLO

Apollo is the god of the sun, music, poetry, prophecy, and healing in Greek mythology. He is the son of Zeus and Leto and twin brother of Artemis. Apollo is often depicted as youthful and radiant, carrying a lyre or bow, symbolizing his mastery over music, arts, and archery. He presides over prophecy at Delphi and is also associated with medicine and the ability to bring both disease and health.

  • Roles & Attributes: God of the sun, light, music, poetry, prophecy, and healing. Symbols include the lyre, laurel wreath, bow and arrow, and the sun chariot.
  • Family & Origin: Son of Zeus and Leto; twin brother of Artemis. Part of the twelve Olympian gods.
  • Significant Achievements: Patron of the Oracle at Delphi, protector of young men, inspirer of music and poetry.
