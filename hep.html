HEPHAESTUS

Hephaestus is the god of fire, metalworking, craftsmanship, and blacksmiths in Greek mythology. He is the son of Zeus and Hera and is one of the twelve Olympian gods. Known for his incredible skill in forging weapons, armor, and magical items for both gods and heroes, Hephaestus was the divine craftsman of Mount Olympus. Despite being lame from birth, he was respected for his intelligence, creativity, and unmatched ability in crafting powerful objects.

  • Roles & Attributes: God of fire, metalworking, blacksmiths, and craftsmanship. His symbols include the hammer, anvil, tongs, and fire.
  • Family & Origin: Son of Zeus and Hera (in some myths Hera alone). He was cast from Mount Olympus and later returned because of his incredible craftsmanship.
  • Creations: Forged powerful items such as Achilles' armor, Hermes' winged helmet, and many tools and weapons used by the gods.
