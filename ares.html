ARES

Ares is the Greek god of war, representing the brutal and chaotic aspect of battle. Unlike Athena, who symbolizes strategy and wisdom in warfare, Ares embodies raw violence, bloodshed, and physical courage. Though a son of Zeus and Hera, he was often disliked by both gods and mortals for his aggressive and destructive nature.

  • Roles & Attributes: Ares is the god of war, bloodlust, and courage. His symbols include the spear, helmet, shield, dog, and vulture. He is often depicted as a strong warrior in full battle armor.
  • Family & Origin: He is the son of Zeus and Hera and brother to Hebe and Eileithyia. Despite being an Olympian, he was not as respected as other gods due to his violent temperament.
  • Children: Ares fathered several children, including Phobos (fear), Deimos (terror), Harmonia, and the Amazon warriors in some myths.
