    In the kingdom of Calydon there was born a prince named Meleager, son of King Oeneus and Queen Althaea. On the night of his birth, as the palace slept, three mysterious Fates appeared beside his cradle. They gazed upon the newborn child and spoke of his destiny. Clotho spun the thread of his life. Lachesis measured its length. But Atropos, the most feared, pointed to a small log burning in the hearth and declared that Meleager would live only as long as that piece of wood remained unburned. Horrified, Queen Althaea leapt from her bed and snatched the log from the flames. She extinguished it at once and hid it away in a chest of cedar, guarding it with desperate love. Thus Meleager grew into a powerful warrior — brave, swift, and noble — unaware that his life was bound to a single piece of wood. Years passed, and Calydon prospered. But fate was not finished with him. One year, King Oeneus forgot to honor Artemis, goddess of the hunt, during the sacred harvest offerings. The insult enraged her. In her fury, she sent a monstrous boar to ravage the lands of Calydon.

    The beast was enormous, with tusks like curved swords and bristles sharp as spears. It trampled fields, destroyed crops, and gored any who dared approach. To save his people, Meleager called together the greatest heroes of Greece to hunt the beast. Among them was the fierce huntress Atalanta, whose skill with the bow rivaled any man’s. The hunt was brutal. Many warriors were wounded. Some were slain. At last, Atalanta struck the first wound upon the boar with a perfectly aimed arrow. Seizing the moment, Meleager drove his spear deep into the creature’s heart. The monstrous beast fell. Victory belonged to them both. Honoring Atalanta’s bravery, Meleager awarded her the prized hide of the boar. But his uncles, angered that a woman should receive such glory, seized it from her by force. Fury ignited within Meleager. In a moment of rage, he struck them down. News of the deaths reached Queen Althaea. When she learned that her own son had killed her brothers, grief and vengeance battled within her heart. Love for her child warred against loyalty to her blood. In her anguish, she remembered the hidden log — the fragile key to Meleager’s life. For hours she wept beside the chest. But sorrow hardened into wrath.

    Thus the royal house of Calydon was shattered by grief — not by monsters, nor by gods alone, but by the terrible power of fate and anger. Meleager was remembered as a hero who saved his kingdom from destruction, yet whose life was bound to a fragile thread from the very beginning. His story became a warning whispered through generations: That destiny cannot be escaped, that wrath can consume even love, and that even the mightiest warrior may fall not by sword — but by the hand of fate itself. When Meleager was still an infant, the Fates — the Moirai — declared that he would live only as long as a single log in the hearth remained unburned. His mother, Althaea, snatched the wood from the flames and concealed it away, saving her child’s life by extinguishing the fire. She sealed it inside a chest and guarded it like the most precious treasure, for within that simple piece of timber rested her son’s very existence. Freed from immediate doom, Meleager grew into greatness. He rose to become one of the finest heroes of his generation, joining noble warriors in the hunt for the savage beast sent by Artemis as punishment upon King Oeneus.

    It was Meleager’s strength and valor that helped bring the creature down, rescuing Calydon from devastation. Yet even in victory, destruction waited. When Meleager chose to honor the huntress Atalanta with the spoils of the hunt, his uncles felt insulted and enraged. Pride flared. Harsh words turned to violence. In the heat of anger, Meleager struck them down.
