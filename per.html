PERSEPHONE

Persephone is the Greek goddess of spring, vegetation, and the queen of the underworld. She is the daughter of Demeter and Zeus. Persephone is most famous for the myth in which Hades abducted her to be his queen in the underworld. Her annual return to the surface brings spring and the growth of plants, while her descent marks the arrival of winter.

  • Roles & Attributes: Goddess of spring, vegetation, and queen of the underworld. Her symbols include pomegranate, flowers, and torch.
  • Family & Origin: Daughter of Demeter and Zeus.
  • Myth: Her yearly return from the underworld explains the changing seasons.
