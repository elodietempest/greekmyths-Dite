AENEAS

Aeneas is a Trojan hero and demigod in Greek and Roman mythology, son of Anchises and the goddess Aphrodite. He fought bravely in the Trojan War and is most famous for surviving the fall of Troy and leading the survivors on a journey that eventually led to the founding of Rome. Aeneas is celebrated for his piety, courage, and sense of duty.

  • Roles & Attributes: Demigod hero, Trojan warrior, founder figure of Rome, known for piety and bravery.
  • Family & Origin: Son of Anchises and Aphrodite.
  • Myth: Survived the Trojan War and journeyed to Italy, laying the foundation for the Roman people.
