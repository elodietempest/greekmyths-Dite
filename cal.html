CALYPSO

Calypso is a nymph in Greek mythology who lived on the island of Ogygia. She is known for her beauty and magical powers. Calypso is most famous for keeping the hero Odysseus on her island for several years during his journey home from the Trojan War. She cared for him and offered him immortality if he stayed with her, but eventually she was ordered by the gods to let him leave.

  • Roles & Attributes: Sea nymph associated with the island of Ogygia, known for her beauty, charm, and magical abilities.
  • Family & Origin: Often said to be the daughter of the Titan Atlas.
  • Myth: Famous for keeping Odysseus on her island for seven years before the gods commanded her to release him.
