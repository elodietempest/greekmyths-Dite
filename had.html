HADES

Hades is the Greek god of the Underworld and ruler of the realm of the dead. Unlike his brothers Zeus and Poseidon, Hades did not dwell on Mount Olympus but governed the shadowy kingdom beneath the earth. Though often misunderstood as evil, he was a stern yet just ruler who maintained balance between life and death.

  • Roles & Attributes: Hades is the god of the Underworld, the dead, and hidden wealth. His symbols include the bident (two-pronged spear), the helm of invisibility, the black ram, and Cerberus, the three-headed guardian of the gates.
  • Family & Origin: He is the son of Cronus and Rhea and brother to Zeus, Poseidon, Hera, Demeter, and Hestia. After the Titanomachy, he drew lots with his brothers and received dominion over the Underworld.
  • Consort & Realm: Hades is married to Persephone, queen of the Underworld. His realm contains the River Styx, the Fields of Asphodel, Elysium, and Tartarus.
