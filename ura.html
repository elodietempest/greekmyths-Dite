URANUS

Uranus is the primordial Greek god of the sky and one of the first beings in Greek mythology. He is the son and husband of Gaia (Earth) and the father of the Titans, Cyclopes, and Hecatoncheires. Uranus represents the heavens above the Earth and was overthrown by his son Cronus, marking the beginning of the Titan era.

  • Roles & Attributes: Primordial god of the sky, father of the Titans, Cyclopes, and Hecatoncheires.
  • Family & Origin: Son and consort of Gaia (Earth), father of Cronus, Rhea, Oceanus, Coeus, Crius, Hyperion, and others.
  • Myth: Overthrown by his son Cronus, who castrated him at Gaia’s urging.
