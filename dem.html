DEMETER

Demeter is the Greek goddess of agriculture, harvest, and fertility of the earth. She is one of the twelve Olympian gods and is responsible for the growth of crops and the nourishment of humanity. Demeter was widely worshipped by farmers and was closely connected to the changing of the seasons through the story of her daughter Persephone.

  • Roles & Attributes: Goddess of agriculture, harvest, fertility, and the earth. Her symbols include wheat, cornucopia, torch, and bread.
  • Family & Origin: Daughter of the Titans Cronus and Rhea, and sister of Zeus, Hera, Poseidon, Hades, and Hestia.
  • Children: Mother of Persephone, whose story explains the cycle of the seasons when she spends part of the year in the underworld.
