ARTEMIS

Artemis is the Greek goddess of the hunt, wilderness, wild animals, and the moon. She is one of the twelve Olympian gods and the twin sister of Apollo. Artemis is known for her independence, strength, and skill as a hunter. She protected nature, young women, and animals, and was often worshipped by hunters and travelers.

  • Roles & Attributes: Goddess of the hunt, wilderness, animals, and the moon. Her symbols include the bow and arrow, deer, moon, and cypress tree.
  • Family & Origin: Daughter of Zeus and Leto, and twin sister of Apollo.
  • Characteristics: Known for her independence and strength, Artemis chose to remain unmarried and protect nature and young women.
