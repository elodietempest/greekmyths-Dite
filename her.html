HERA

Hera is the queen of the gods in Greek mythology and the goddess of marriage, family, and women. She is the wife and sister of Zeus and one of the twelve Olympian gods. Hera is often portrayed as regal and powerful, protecting marriage and punishing those who betrayed it. She was widely worshipped throughout ancient Greece and was considered the protector of married women.

  • Roles & Attributes: Goddess of marriage, family, childbirth, and queenship. Her symbols include the peacock, crown, pomegranate, and cow.
  • Family & Origin: Daughter of the Titans Cronus and Rhea, sister and wife of Zeus. She was swallowed by Cronus at birth and later freed by Zeus.
  • Children: Mother of Ares, Hephaestus, Hebe, and Eileithyia.
